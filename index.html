The Challenge

When I joined Calbright, the college had approximately 1,500 enrolled students. Through integrated digital strategy — encompassing UX improvements, content strategy, targeted email campaigns, and data-driven enrollment marketing — the institution grew to over 8,000 enrolled students across the state of California. The digital experience had to earn trust fast, reduce confusion, and create clear pathways to enrollment for users with high skepticism and limited time.

UX Process
01Behavioral research & accessibility audit — analyzed drop-off data, support ticket themes, and usability gaps to identify where learners were losing confidence or path.
02WCAG accessibility remediation — directed compliance improvements across learner-facing platforms, prioritizing mobile usability and plain-language content rewrites.
03Content strategy overhaul — restructured program pages around learner questions ("What will I do? What does it cost? How long?") rather than institutional descriptions.
04Email program development — built and executed multi-campaign email strategy across enrollment, program launches, and student engagement, establishing consistent brand voice and measurable performance benchmarks.
05Analytics infrastructure — implemented SEO, SEM, and CRM frameworks enabling data-driven, iterative product improvement and ongoing performance measurement.

Kendall College was acquired by National Louis University in 2018. The site shown reflects the brand and navigational architecture from the 2005–2008 redesign period.

The Challenge

Kendall's website was organized around academic departments — how the college thought about itself — rather than how prospective students made decisions. Enrollment was flat. The task was a full rethinking of the digital experience, from information architecture through content strategy, alongside an institutional brand repositioning.

UX Process
01Prospective student journey mapping — identified how students actually made program decisions and where the site created friction or confusion along that path.
02Information architecture redesign — restructured navigation and program pages around user goals ("I want a culinary career") rather than the institution's internal org structure.
03Content strategy & faculty collaboration — partnered with deans and faculty to translate academic strengths into student-facing narratives grounded in outcomes and career paths.
04Developer & designer direction — directed design and development through the full build: reviewing prototypes and staging, and iterating based on behavioral data post-launch.

The opportunity was in community trust and local relevance — building a digital presence that felt genuinely connected to the communities it served, not just a regional branch of something larger.

03Community content strategy — developed digital content grounded in local relationships, civic partnerships, and community stories — differentiating the brand through authentic local relevance.

How I Work

A consistent process
across every institution

Whether the product is a flagship university site or a single program page, the process stays the same: understand before building, involve the right people, never stop measuring.

1
Understand the user first
Behavioral data, drop-off analysis, and real user feedback — before touching design or content. The user's reality is the starting constraint, not an afterthought.
2
Align institutional priorities
User needs and business goals aren't always in conflict — but when they are, I bring evidence into the room. Consensus built on data holds longer than consensus built on opinion.
3
Build with the technical team
Requirements that include the "why." Prototype reviews before final build. Pushback when something technically correct doesn't solve the user's actual problem.
4
Measure, learn, improve
Launch is not the end. Post-launch data informs the next iteration — making every product more useful each cycle rather than waiting for the next big redesign.
